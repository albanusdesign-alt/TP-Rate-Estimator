Effective Rate — The Core Move
Total fees ÷ total volume = effective rate.

Most owners only know their advertised rate — not what they actually pay after batch fees, PCI fees, monthly minimums, and statement fees stack on top. The gap between those two numbers is your opening.

Example: Owner thinks they're on 2.2%. You pull the statement and the math shows 3.1%. That's your moment.
Statement Review — What to Look For
  • Total monthly volume processed
  • Total fees charged (all line items)
  • Are they locked in a contract? When does it end?
  • Who is their current processor?
⚠ Reading a Surcharge Statement
On a surcharge or dual pricing statement, "Total Fees" includes money the customer paid — not the shop. The real business cost is the End of Month Charge / Month-End Debit line. Quoting off Total Fees overstates their cost by 4x or more. Always point to the month-end debit.
